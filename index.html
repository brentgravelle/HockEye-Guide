Part 1: Team Setup (Owner/Admin)

This section covers the core administrative features for managing your team's information, roster, and schedule.

Team Info

This is where you set your team's identity.

  • Team Details: Add your team's city and name, upload a logo, and set your primary, secondary, and tertiary team colors.
  • Use Lines: Toggling this "On" makes the Lines tab available in the Team Manager. This provides an alternative, visual way for coaches to set the game roster using line combinations.
  • GameTracker Settings: This allows you to enable or disable the tracking of optional events like Hits, Blocks, Takeaways, etc. Mandatory events (Shots, Goals, Misses, Posts) cannot be disabled.
  • League Integration: This feature allows you to specify your league's standings webpage URL. When set, the standings will be displayed embedded directly within the HockEye site.
üí° Pro Tip: When your team is at a tournament, you can temporarily change the Standings Page URL to the tournament's website to easily keep track of the standings.

Roster

Manage your list of players for the season.

  • Player Creation: Add new players by providing their jersey number, name, and position. You can also add detailed contact and biographical information.
  • Player Analytics: Click on any player to view their detailed analytics. The data shown is based on the currently selected game filters (e.g., Full Season, Last 5 Games).
  • Import Roster: Quickly populate your roster by uploading a CSV file. The app uses smart logic to automatically map columns like "Jersey #", "Last Name", etc., and allows you to review and adjust mappings before the final import.
Roster & Player Analytics View

Staff

Manage your coaches, managers, and other team staff. Granting staff access is a two-step process.

Step 1: Add a Staff Member

First, add the individual to your staff list by providing their name and role (e.g., Head Coach, Manager). At this stage, they are listed as staff but do not yet have access to the app.

Step 2: Grant App Access

After adding the staff member, edit their record to grant them access to the HockEye application. You will assign them one of the following roles:

  • Assistant Coach: Has full access to manage the team, schedule, roster, and track games. Can also view all analytics.
  • Tracker: Can track games using GameTracker and view analytics, but cannot manage the team roster or schedule.
  • Viewer: Has read-only access to view stats and shot charts. Cannot track games or manage the team.

Once access is granted, the staff member can sign up on the HockEye website using the same email address. They will automatically be given access to your team with their assigned role.

Staff Management and Access Roles

Lines

The Lines manager is a visual tool primarily used as an alternative way for coaches to set the game roster. There is currently no data or tracking associated with specific line combinations.

  • Current vs. Default: "Current Lines" are the lines for a specific, upcoming game. "Default Lines" are your go-to combinations that you can quickly load into the "Current" view to save time.
  • Handedness Indicator: If a player's handedness (Left/Right) is defined in their roster profile, the edges of their player box in the Lines manager will be highlighted in blue to reflect that.
Lines Manager

Schedule

Manage your team's calendar of games for the season.

  • Add/Import Games: You can add games manually one by one, or import an entire season's schedule from a CSV file using the smart mapping tool.
  • Set Game Roster: Before a game, you must set the roster. You can do this from the schedule by either using the Lines manager (if enabled) or the standard Roster screen, where non-affiliated players are automatically pre-selected.
  • Finalize (Lock/Unlock) Games: After a game is tracked, it should be "Finalized". This locks the game from further edits and makes its data available in the aggregate stats views. A coach or owner can unlock a game if corrections are needed.

Part 2: GameTracker Guide

This tool is designed to help you capture important game events quickly and accurately, providing valuable data for your coaches and players.

Important First Step: Team Settings

Before you start tracking, it's crucial to understand that the features available within GameTracker are controlled by the Team Management settings. Your team owner can decide which events to track (e.g., Hits, Blocks, Assists). If you don't see an option for an action, it's likely disabled in the team settings.

Team Management Settings

Pre-Game Setup

Follow these steps before the puck drops to ensure a smooth tracking experience.

1. Select Your Game

From the main dropdown menu, choose the game you are about to track. Only games that have not been finalized will appear here.

Game Selection Dropdown
Already Set Up? If you select a game and are taken directly to the rink without seeing the popups below, it means the coach has already set the roster and goalie. You can proceed directly to tracking!

2. Set the Game Roster

When you select a game for the first time, a Roster Setup screen will appear. You can select primary and affiliate players. Click "Set Roster" to confirm.

Note: You can add players to the roster mid-game, but you cannot remove them. This must be done by the account owner.
Roster Setup Screen

3. Select the Starting Goalie

After the roster is set, you must select the starting goalie or "Empty Net". Tracking cannot begin until a goalie is selected.

Goalie Selection

The Tracking Interface

The main screen consists of three key areas: the Rink, the Controls, and the Events List.

Note on Layouts: Depending on your device and screen size, the GameTracker display may look slightly different.
  • On wider screens, the Events List appears beside the rink.
  • On tablets or smaller laptops, the Events List may appear below the rink.
  • Mobile phones use a completely different "Focus Mode" optimized for touchscreens.

Tracking Shots, Goals, Misses, and Posts

Part 3: Shot Chart

The Shot Chart provides a visual representation of all shot-related events for a selected game.

  • Flip View: If the person tracking the game was on the opposite side of the ice from the coach's perspective, the Flip View button will correct the orientation, making the chart easier to analyze.
  • Dynamic Filtering: The filter options (Periods, Actions, Game Situation) are dynamic. They only show options that are relevant to the data in the selected game. For example, if no penalties were recorded, "Power Play" will not appear as a filter option.
  • Action Details: Clicking on any action's icon on the rink or net will highlight it and display its details, such as the player, period, and game situation.
Shot Chart Interface

Part 4: Stats

The Stats section provides detailed breakdowns of team and player performance.

  • Data Aggregation: You can view stats for the full season, the last number of games, a specific game, or by game type (e.g., Regular Season, Playoffs).
  • Danger Zones: Shots are categorized into High, Mid, and Low danger zones based on their location on the ice. This helps coaches understand the quality of chances for and against.

Glossary of Terms

Game Situation
Refers to the on-ice strength of the teams, such as 5v5 (even strength), 5v4 (power play), or 4v5 (penalty kill).
Offsetting Penalty
When a player from each team receives a penalty at the same time, resulting in no change to the on-ice player count (e.g., play remains 5v5).
Finalize
The act of locking a game's data after it has concluded. This prevents further edits and makes the game's statistics available for analysis.
