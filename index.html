Important First Step: Team Management Settings

Before you start tracking, it's crucial to understand that the features available within GameTracker are controlled by the Team Management settings. Your team owner can decide which events to track (e.g., Hits, Blocks, Assists). If you don't see an option for an action, it's likely disabled in the team settings.

Part 1: Pre-Game Setup

Follow these steps before the puck drops to ensure a smooth tracking experience.

1. Select Your Game

From the main dropdown menu, choose the game you are about to track. Only games that have not been finalized will appear here.

Game Selection Dropdown
Already Set Up? If you select a game and are taken directly to the rink without seeing the popups below, it means the coach has already set the roster and goalie. You can proceed directly to tracking!

2. Set the Game Roster

When you select a game for the first time, a Roster Setup screen will appear. You can select primary and affiliate players. Click "Set Roster" to confirm.

Note: You can add players to the roster mid-game, but you cannot remove them. This must be done by the account owner.
Roster Setup Screen

3. Select the Starting Goalie

After the roster is set, you must select the starting goalie or "Empty Net". Tracking cannot begin until a goalie is selected.

Goalie Selection

Part 2: The Tracking Interface

The main screen consists of three key areas: the Rink, the Controls, and the Events List.

Note on Layouts: Depending on your device and screen size, the GameTracker display may look slightly different.
  • On wider screens, the Events List appears beside the rink.
  • On tablets or smaller laptops, the Events List may appear below the rink.
  • Mobile phones use a completely different "Focus Mode" optimized for touchscreens.

Part 3: How to Track Events

Tracking follows a simple workflow: Click the Rink ‚Üí Select the Player ‚Üí Select the Action.

Tracking Shots, Goals, Misses, and Posts

After clicking the rink, select the player who performed the action. Choose a player from your team, or select the red 'Opponent' button to track any event caused by the other team.

Next, choose the shot type (SHOT, GOAL, MISS, or POST). If "Shot Location" is enabled in your team's settings, a Net Location popup will then appear. Click on the net where the shot went to record its location.

Tracking Faceoffs

  1. Click the dedicated Faceoff button.
  2. Select the player taking the faceoff.
  3. An overlay with WIN/LOSS buttons will appear over the 5 main faceoff zones.
  4. Click WIN or LOSS in the appropriate zone to record the outcome.

All neutral zone faceoffs should be recorded using the center ice WIN/LOSS buttons.

Automatic Zone Detection: As your team and the opponent take shots, GameTracker will automatically learn which end is your offensive zone and which is your defensive zone for the period. Faceoff events will then be updated to reflect this (e.g., "OFF FO", "DEF FO").
Faceoff Overlay

Tracking Penalties & Game Situations

When you log a penalty, the Game Situation will automatically change. However, you must manually change it back when the penalty expires.

Part 5: After the Game

Finalizing a Game

Once the game is over, click the "Finalize" button in the events table header. This locks the game from further editing and makes it available in Analytics. A coach or owner can un-finalize it if changes are needed later.

Part 6: Strategies for Effective Tracking

üí° Pro Tip: Batch Your Events! During heavy action, remember 2-3 events. At the next whistle, quickly log all of them. Accuracy is more important than real-time speed.

Collaborative Tracking (Team Tracking)

It's possible for multiple people to track the same game simultaneously to capture more detail. If you use this method, divide the responsibilities clearly before the game starts.

Example Division of Labor:

  • Tracker 1 (Primary): Focuses on all shot events (Shots, Goals, Misses, Posts) and Faceoffs.
  • Tracker 2 (Secondary): Focuses on other events like Takeaways, Giveaways, Hits, and Blocks.

When one person enters a penalty, the game situation (e.g., 5v4 PP) updates for everyone tracking the game. This ensures all events are marked with the proper on-ice situation, which is vital for accurate analysis. Remember to also manually reset the game situation when the penalty expires.

Glossary of Terms

Game Situation
Refers to the on-ice strength of the teams, such as 5v5 (even strength), 5v4 (power play), or 4v5 (penalty kill).
Offsetting Penalty
When a player from each team receives a penalty at the same time, resulting in no change to the on-ice player count (e.g., play remains 5v5).
Finalize
The act of locking a game's data after it has concluded. This prevents further edits and makes the game's statistics available for analysis.
